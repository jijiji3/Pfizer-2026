Weekly Schedule · Strength, Recovery & Mobility
YOUR TRAINING
FRAMEWORK
Each workout in this schedule supports your running — building strength, improving mobility, aiding recovery, and sharpening performance for race day. Follow the links to your curated video sessions.
How Your Workouts Serve Your Running
🏋️
Monday Upper Body + Core + Plyometrics
Running benefit: power, posture, arm drive
Upper body and core strength directly improves your running economy. A strong core stabilises your pelvis mid-race, reducing energy leakage and preventing the lateral sway that creeps in after kilometre 14. Plyometrics on Monday (fresh legs, furthest from your Thursday LT session) builds reactive strength and improves your foot-ground contact time — a key factor in 4:58/km pace efficiency.
⚡
Do plyos at the start when CNS is fresh — box jumps, broad jumps, single-leg hops before the core work
🎯
Foot rollers post-workout helps plantar fascia health — essential for half marathon mileage
🔗
Mobility work on Monday prepares your hips and ankles for Tuesday's easy run with strides
🦵
Wednesday Lower Body / Single-Leg Focus
Running benefit: hip stability, glute activation, injury prevention
Single-leg strength work is one of the highest-value investments a runner can make. Running is entirely single-leg — every stride is a single-leg squat and landing. Strengthening glutes medius (hip abductors), hip extensors, and calf complex reduces injury risk and improves propulsion. Scheduled on Wednesday (between Tuesday easy and Thursday LT), it ensures your legs are recovered for quality work.
⚡
Prioritise: Bulgarian split squats, single-leg RDLs, lateral band walks, step-ups with knee drive
🎯
Keep weight moderate — you want muscle fatigue, not the DOMS that ruins your Thursday session
🔗
Calf raises (single-leg) directly reduce Achilles load during your high mileage weeks
🔥
Thursday LT / Tempo Session
Running benefit: raise lactate threshold → sustain 4:58/km
This is the engine of your plan. Every Thursday LT session trains your body to clear lactate faster at race pace. Over 24 weeks, you're progressively running longer at harder paces (6:20/km → 5:00/km) — shifting your threshold upward. On race day, what once felt like a 7/10 effort at 5:00/km becomes a 6/10. That is the entire game.
⚡
Effort should feel "comfortably hard" — you can speak short sentences but not hold full conversation
🎯
Nail the warm-up km. Never start cold on LT work — 2 km easy, dynamic drills, then threshold
🔗
Log every LT session on Garmin and share with Coach for trend analysis across the 24 weeks
💆
Friday Recovery + Resistance Bands + Massage Gun
Running benefit: flush Thursday's LT stress, prep for the weekend
Friday is a strategic reset. After Thursday's LT stress, your muscles need blood flow and parasympathetic nervous system activation — not more load. Light resistance bands (glute activation, hip external rotation) maintain neuromuscular patterns without fatiguing. The massage gun targets calves, quads, and IT band — the areas under most stress during tempo running.
⚡
Elliptical option: 20–25 min easy (RPE 4–5) maintains aerobic stimulus without impact stress
🎯
Massage gun order: calves → hamstrings → quads → glutes → 2 min each
🧘
Saturday Pilates + Easy / Parkrun
Running benefit: deep core stability, hip flexor length, posture
Pilates provides the core and postural work that traditional strength training doesn't reach — specifically the deep stabilisers (transverse abdominis, multifidus) that hold your running form together in the final kilometres. Paired with an easy parkrun or short run, Saturday builds aerobic base while the Pilates keeps you aligned and injury-resilient.
⚡
Pilates before or after parkrun? After — run first while legs are fresh, then Pilates as active recovery
🎯
In peak weeks (15–21), treat Parkrun as a controlled fitness test at 5:10–5:20/km, not all-out
